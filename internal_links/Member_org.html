Association of Indian Students

We at Association of Indian Students indulge in everything Indian you bring, and locate amidst our diversities what we all have in common. We dance away to desi beats, splash colors on our faces, gulp those gol gappas off a saucer, sing the national anthem, and watch with bated breath as Dhoni smashes that last ball into the stands. And until the summons from home becomes too loud to ignore, we keep you connected to your roots.

Chinese Student Association

It is our desire to create a cohesive environment, with an aim to foster a sense of belonging for international students, enhance diversity and promulgate Chinese culture and heritage within the University community.

Burmese Students Association of USC

BURSA is committed to foster a sense of community between the Burmese students of USC as well as encourage interaction our members with other international and local students to raise awareness of the culture of Myanmar.

Australia New Zealand Student Association

The purpose of ANSA is to engage and promote students from the regions of Australia and New Zealand as well as create a social environment for these students to be involved in.

Association of Indonesian Students

Our goals include gathering as many people who are interested in Indonesian culture to create a community here at USC, as well as raising awareness of Indonesian culture to the USC student body. ISA can help achieve this, as with its wide reach of international students at USC it can help gather students and promote our club as well; furthermore, with close interactions with other member organizations it will also open a path for collaboration among ISA members.

Brazilian Student Association

Our organization’s mission is to promote Brazilian culture, empower its members, and serve as an official linkage between USC and Brazilians.

Chinese Students & Scholars Association

University of Southern California Chinese Students and Scholars Association (USC CSSA) is a nonprofit, student-run organization that targets the Chinese community at USC. Our mission is to provide assistance to Chinese students and scholars in areas like studies, career development and recreation, to promote Chinese culture, and to facilitate communications between the Chinese community and other local communities.

Hong Kong Students’ Association

The Hong Kong Students Association at the University of Southern California is a regional student organization established for over twenty years, with an objective of uniting Hong Kong students within the university for a greater cause. HKSA organizes a multitude of activities and special events to provide its members with a “home away from home”. In cooperation with other universities and organizations abroad, we also host joint university events aiming to further strengthen the bond among all Hong Kong students across the globe.

Japanese Student Association

The purpose of JSA is to promote the interchange of Japanese culture among the USC community through social and professional activities. JSA will promote the exchange of cultures among Japanese and non-Japanese students at social events, and assist career development by providing a link between Japanese companies and USC students at professional events.

Korean Business Student Organization

Korean Business Student Organization (KBSO) is a Marshall School recognized student volunteer group that aims to better both the collective and individual futures of Korean international students at the University of Southern California. The chief mission of KBSO is to assist our target students towards their respective professional lives – we achieve this end year after year by bridging students to the companies of their choice.

Korean International Student Association

KISA has two missions: one is to spread the essence of traditional Korean culture to the USC campus student environment. The other is to help Korean international students adjust to the college/American life (as most have never lived in the US) in multiple aspects.

Pan African Student Association

The purpose of this organization shall be to build a community that allows students interested in the Pan-African experience to engage in meaningful interactions geared towards the furtherance of social, educational and cultural growth within the USC Pan-African Community.

Thai Student Association

Develop strong networks among Thai Trojans as well as foreign members. Raise awareness of Thai culture and traditions on USC campus.

Trogons A Cappella

Trogons provides all USC students, regardless of the nationality, ethnicity of the individuals, the chance to explore the culture of East Asia through song and music.

USC Lebanese Club

To promote Lebanese culture and traditions among USC students through cultural and social activities. To act as a vehicle for expressing Lebanese student concerns and interests to the university community. Considering the sizeable yet dispersed Lebanese population at USC, our goal is to provide a hub where all these students can meet, celebrate their culture, and ultimately find their community on campus. The Lebanese club will aid both incoming international Lebanese students as well as local ones more easily adapt to USC.

Taiwanese Student Association

The purpose of TSA is to motivate Taiwanese students get involved in USC culture and activities, to promote the awareness of Taiwanese culture to the entire student body, to strengthen students’ voice on/beyond the campus, to connect with alumni, as well as to build lasting friendships.
